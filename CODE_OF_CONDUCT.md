# Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

UIAudit.js is committed to providing a welcoming, inclusive, and harassment-free environment for all community members, regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, gender identity and expression, level of experience, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, sexual identity and orientation, or socioeconomic status.

We are dedicated to creating a respectful and professional community where everyone feels safe, valued, and empowered to contribute their best work.

## Our Standards

### Expected Behavior

Community members are expected to:

- **Be Respectful**: Treat all individuals with dignity and respect
- **Be Inclusive**: Welcome and support people of all backgrounds and identities
- **Be Collaborative**: Work together constructively to achieve common goals
- **Be Professional**: Maintain a high standard of professionalism in all interactions
- **Be Patient**: Understand that people have different perspectives and learning curves
- **Give Credit**: Acknowledge the contributions and ideas of others
- **Listen Actively**: Seek to understand different viewpoints before responding
- **Assume Good Intent**: Interpret comments and actions charitably when possible

### Unacceptable Behavior

The following behaviors are strictly prohibited and will not be tolerated:

- **Harassment**: Any form of harassment based on protected characteristics or other factors
- **Discrimination**: Exclusion or prejudice based on identity, background, or status
- **Abuse**: Verbal, physical, or written abuse, threats, or intimidation
- **Hate Speech**: Slurs, derogatory language, or expressions promoting discrimination
- **Sexual Harassment**: Unwelcome sexual advances, requests, or other conduct of a sexual nature
- **Trolling**: Deliberate attempts to provoke or upset community members
- **Doxxing**: Sharing or threatening to share personal information without consent
- **Spam**: Repeated unsolicited messages or content
- **Plagiarism**: Taking credit for others' work or ideas
- **Breach of Privacy**: Sharing confidential information or private conversations
- **Derailing**: Intentionally steering discussions away from constructive topics

## Our Responsibilities

Project maintainers are responsible for:

- Clarifying the standards of acceptable behavior
- Taking appropriate and fair corrective action in response to violations
- Removing, editing, or rejecting comments, commits, code, wiki edits, issues, and other contributions that violate this Code of Conduct
- Banning temporarily or permanently any contributor for behavior that violates this Code of Conduct
- Maintaining confidentiality regarding reports of violations

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all community interactions, including but not limited to:

- GitHub repositories (issues, pull requests, discussions, comments)
- Commit messages and code reviews
- Email communications
- Discussion forums or chat channels
- Conference talks or presentations
- Social media posts related to the project
- In-person events and meetups

This Code of Conduct applies to both community members and maintainers, and applies even when an individual is not actively participating in the community.

## Reporting Violations

If you witness or experience a violation of this Code of Conduct, please report it immediately:

### How to Report

1. **Email**: Send a detailed report to the project maintainers at [contact email to be determined]
2. **GitHub Issues**: For minor concerns, you may create a private security advisory
3. **Direct Message**: Contact a project maintainer directly on GitHub

### What to Include

When reporting, please provide:

- **Description**: What happened and when
- **People Involved**: Names or usernames of those involved
- **Context**: Links to relevant conversations, issues, or commits
- **Impact**: How this behavior affected you or others
- **Evidence**: Screenshots, logs, or other documentation (if available)
- **Confidentiality Request**: Whether you want your identity kept private

### Confidentiality

All reports will be handled confidentially. We will not share the identity of the reporter without their consent, except as required by law.

## Investigation & Response

### Process

1. **Receipt**: We will acknowledge receipt of your report within 48 hours
2. **Investigation**: We will investigate the report thoroughly and impartially
3. **Decision**: We will determine an appropriate response
4. **Action**: We will take corrective action as necessary
5. **Follow-up**: We will follow up with relevant parties

### Possible Actions

Depending on the severity and nature of the violation, actions may include:

- **Warning**: A formal warning about the behavior
- **Temporary Suspension**: Temporary removal from the community
- **Permanent Ban**: Permanent removal from the community
- **Escalation**: Reporting to relevant authorities if applicable

## Appeals

If you disagree with the outcome of an investigation, you may appeal:

1. Submit a written appeal within 14 days of the decision
2. Include new evidence or information not previously considered
3. Appeals will be reviewed by a different maintainer or team
